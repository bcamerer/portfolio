Microsoft - Iridias

Iridias was an internal app designed to monitor the health of Microsoft's online services. It would provide real time data on a service problem and what the status was on the solution. After targeted user researching and usability testing we found out it had too many features, a confusing UX, and poor aesthetics.

USER RESEARCH FEEDBACK

Don't just design for your users, design with your users. We invited a targeted group of users to test the app and provide usability feedback. They said...

TOO MANY FEATURES

Users expressed there was simply too much going on. They did not need this many attributes to make this app effective for them.

CONFUSING UX

Everything did not have a logical place. Users had to guess where to go or what to do next. They wanted to be shown the most effective route.

POOR AESTHETICS

Yellow floating boxes? The overall visual design needed a serious tune up. It just didn't look and feel like an app that would better their jobs.

USABILITY SOLUTIONS

After unpacking all the user research, we mocked up some usability solutions to ease the frustration and implement our user feedback.

Streamlined Features

After aquiring a significant amount of research from users, we discovered what features they did not use and which ones they did. We were able to target our focus on expanding these features and making them efficient.

Next

Simplified UX

The users were clearly frustrated by the lack of an efficient task flow. We were able to design to their needs in a clean and easy process. They didn't want to have to ponder how to use the app, they just wanted it to flow.

Next

Cleaned Aesthetics

Colors and overarching aethetics have a very important role. We responded to the users wants and made sure the colors being used made sense. Red for service interuption alert, yellow for in the process of being fixed and blue running as normal.

Next

Better Product

Finally, the end result. A clear, streamlined and efficient app. Users were empowered, stakeholders were impressed and I was given the unofficial title of "UX Guru". Before my head gets too big, I still have lots more to learn and am excited to continue the ever evolving process of UX.

Findings

Lessons learned and wisdom gained from the finalized redesign of Microsoft - Irdias' UX.

Keep it simple

Your users do not need 400 features. I know it's exciting to design a new app or program but we need to have a keen sense of user empathy with feature implementation. After all, it's all about the user's experience.

Every team needs a UX designer

I am a firm believer that at every stand up meeting there has to be someone whose full time job is to keep the user the center of everything that happens in the software and app creation world.

Software engineer empathy

Remember, software engineers see their product as their baby. If you come in guns a blazing and shredding someone's app there will be an unecessary amount of push back. Clearly and empathetically help your engineers discover the deeper purpose of their creation, the user's experience.

Stay humble

Walking out of Microsoft with a homerun UX redesign does not make you better than anyone else on your team. Each person added value along the way, whether it was the users providing feedback or the engineers rethinking creations. You were on a team and there is no I in team.
